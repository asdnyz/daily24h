# Daily Briefing: 2025-12-28

Here are the top 5 global tech and AI news stories from the last 24 hours:

1.  **AI Spending Fuels Record Global Tech Debt Issuance:** Global technology companies have significantly increased their debt issuance in 2025, reaching record levels. This surge is driven by the intense competition to build artificial intelligence capacity, forcing even companies with substantial cash reserves to borrow heavily for AI investments. Through the first week of December 2025, global tech firms issued $428.3 billion in bonds, with U.S. companies accounting for the largest portion.

2.  **China Unveils Strict Draft Rules for Human-Like AI:** Beijing has introduced draft regulations for AI systems designed to mimic human interaction, aiming for ethical, secure, and transparent development and deployment. These rules will require users to be informed when interacting with AI and mandate security assessments. The proposals are open for public comment until January 25, and could impact global tech companies operating in China.

3.  **Alphabet Acquires Intersect for $4.75 Billion to Power AI:** Alphabet, Google's parent company, is acquiring Intersect for $4.75 billion to secure renewable energy for its data centers, addressing the soaring electricity demand from AI training and inference. This move highlights how AI is compelling major tech companies to consider themselves energy providers, as AI data centers increasingly reshape power infrastructure.

4.  **Nvidia Partners with AI Chip Startup Groq, Acquires Key Talent:** Nvidia has entered a strategic partnership with AI chip startup Groq, acquiring a non-exclusive license for Groq's AI inference chip technology and bringing in key personnel, including Groq's founder. This move strengthens Nvidia's AI hardware capabilities amid growing competition in the sector.

5.  **Amazon Rolls Out Controversial AI Facial Recognition on Ring Doorbells:** Amazon has launched "Familiar Faces," an optional AI-powered facial recognition feature for its Ring video doorbells and cameras in the U.S. While Amazon states the feature processes data in the cloud without using it for AI model training, it has raised significant privacy concerns and privacy advocates, including the Electronic Frontier Foundation and Senator Ed Markey, are warning of potential surveillance and misuse of biometric data.
