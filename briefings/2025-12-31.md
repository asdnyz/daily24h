Here are the top 5 global tech and AI news stories from the last 24 hours:

### Disney Integrates Generative AI into Core Operating Model
Disney is actively embedding generative AI across its entire operational structure, moving beyond experimental phases to fundamental business integration. This initiative aims to improve efficiency in content creation, post-production, and personalized guest experiences in its theme parks. By centralizing AI development, Disney intends to streamline creative workflows and utilize its extensive intellectual property to train proprietary models, while maintaining strict control over brand integrity and copyright.

### Meta Acquires AI Startup Manus for $2 Billion
Meta has purchased the AI startup Manus, a move that signals the social media giant's continued push to enhance its AI offerings across its platforms. Manus's technology is expected to improve Meta's agentic capabilities, such as task completion and answering questions, potentially increasing user engagement on its platforms. This acquisition is part of Meta's broader strategy to compete with major AI players like OpenAI and Google.

### Google Releases Gemini 3 Flash for High-Speed AI Applications
Google has expanded its AI offerings with the launch of Gemini 3 Flash, a lightweight model designed for speed and efficiency at scale. This new model features a large context window while reducing operational costs, making it ideal for developers needing low-latency responses in real-time applications like live translation and coding assistance. Google aims to intensify competition in the generative AI market with this release.

### Salesforce Re-evaluating Aggressive Generative AI Use Amid Reliability Concerns
Salesforce is reportedly reassessing its extensive use of generative AI due to a year of reliability challenges and declining internal confidence. Senior executives have acknowledged a decrease in trust in large language models, attributed to operational failures and customer complaints. Consequently, the company is scaling back its AI system deployments.

### Microsoft Bets on AI Agents in Windows
Microsoft is integrating AI agents deeply into its Windows operating system, reviving a platform strategy that previously established the PC operating system's dominance. The company is exploring whether this approach can still be effective in the current landscape dominated by mobile phones, browsers, and cloud computing.
