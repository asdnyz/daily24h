### Disney Partners with OpenAI for Generative AI Integration
Disney has announced a significant strategic partnership with OpenAI, making it the first major content owner to license its intellectual property for Sora, OpenAI's generative AI video platform. This collaboration allows Sora to create short videos using Disney characters and elements from its franchises, with select fan-inspired videos to be streamed on Disney+.

### China Accelerates AI Development with Over 700 Generative AI Products
China has seen rapid growth in its artificial intelligence sector, with over 700 generative AI large model products completing official filing procedures. This surge reflects advancements in AI, integrated circuits, and software, contributing to the nation's technological independence amidst global chip restrictions.

### Nvidia Faces Demand for AI Chips Amidst Chinese Restrictions
Despite Beijing's encouragement for domestic chip production, Chinese tech giants are still seeking Nvidia's advanced AI chips. Nvidia's H20 model has regained export approval, and the company is developing a more powerful B30A chip, highlighting the ongoing tension between global demand and China's regulatory landscape.

### Memory Chip Demand Surges, Propelling Kioxia Holdings to Top Stock Performance
The escalating demand for memory chips, driven by the AI boom, has led to unprecedented stock gains for Japanese memory chipmaker Kioxia Holdings. This surge underscores the critical role of memory in AI infrastructure and data centers, with Kioxia's shares outperforming global and domestic benchmarks.
