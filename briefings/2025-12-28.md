# Daily Briefing: 2025-12-28

Here are five top global tech and AI news stories from the last 24 hours:

1.  **Mistral AI Secures Significant Funding, Bolstering Europe's AI Ambitions:** French AI startup Mistral AI has raised €1.7 billion (approximately $2 billion) in a Series C funding round led by ASML. This investment values the company at €11.7 billion, making it Europe's most valuable AI startup and highlighting the continent's drive to compete with major US AI players.

2.  **Nebius Signs Massive AI Infrastructure Deal with Microsoft:** AI infrastructure provider Nebius has entered into a five-year agreement with Microsoft worth $17.4 billion, potentially extending to $19.4 billion. This deal will see Nebius supply advanced GPU infrastructure crucial for training large-scale AI models.

3.  **China Unveils Strict Draft Regulations for Human-Like AI:** China's Cyberspace Administration (CAC) has released draft rules for AI systems designed to mimic human interaction. These regulations aim to ensure ethical, secure, and transparent development and deployment, requiring users to be informed when interacting with AI.

4.  **YouTube Grapples with "AI Slop" Content:** A recent study indicates that over 20% of videos recommended to new YouTube users are "AI slop"—low-quality, AI-generated content optimized for views. These channels have garnered billions of views and significant revenue, raising concerns about content saturation on social media platforms.

5.  **Tech Layoffs Continue, AI Talent Bubble May Be Popping:** While overall tech industry layoffs for 2025 are projected to be lower than in 2024, the AI talent market is showing signs of cooling. Reports suggest that companies are re-evaluating their aggressive hiring practices from previous years, with some firms even freezing AI hiring after extensive poaching.
